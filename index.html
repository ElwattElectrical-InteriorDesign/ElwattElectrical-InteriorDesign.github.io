How Much Does House Wiring Cost in Kenya?

A comprehensive guide to understanding wiring costs for different house sizes in Kenya. Budget from KSh 50,000 to KSh 300,000+.

Factors Affecting Cost:

  • House size and number of rooms
  • Quality of materials (cables, switches, sockets)
  • Type of wiring (surface vs concealed)
  • Location and accessibility
  • Additional features (smart switches, surge protection)

Estimated Costs:

  • Bedsitter: KSh 15,000 – 30,000
  • 1-2 Bedroom: KSh 30,000 – 80,000
  • 3-4 Bedroom: KSh 80,000 – 200,000
  • 5+ Bedroom/Mansion: KSh 200,000+

Always get quotes from certified electricians. At Elwatt, we provide free, detailed quotations with no hidden costs.

5 Benefits of Solar Energy for Kenyan Homes

Why more Kenyan families are switching to solar power and how much you can save on monthly bills.

  1. Reduced Electricity Bills: Save 50-80% on monthly power costs.
  2. Energy Independence: No more relying on KPLC and dealing with blackouts.
  3. Environmental Impact: Clean, renewable energy reduces your carbon footprint.
  4. Property Value: Solar installations increase your home's resale value.
  5. Government Incentives: Kenya offers tax benefits for solar adopters.

A 3kW system costs KSh 250,000–400,000 and pays for itself within 3-4 years through savings.

Interior Design Tips for Small Homes

Maximize style and functionality in compact Kenyan apartments with these proven design strategies.

  • Use mirrors: They create the illusion of more space and reflect natural light.
  • Go vertical: Wall shelves and tall storage maximize floor space.
  • Light colors: White and pastels make rooms feel larger and airier.
  • Multi-functional furniture: Ottoman storage, sofa beds, nesting tables.
  • Smart lighting: Layer ambient, task, and accent lights for depth.
Safety 3 min read

Electrical Safety Tips Every Homeowner Should Know

Protect your family with these essential electrical safety practices for Kenyan households.

  • Don't overload sockets: Use one plug per socket and avoid extension cascading.
  • Check wiring regularly: Old or damaged wiring causes fires. Schedule annual inspections.
  • Install RCDs: Residual Current Devices protect against electric shock.
  • Keep water away: Never use electrical appliances near water sources.
  • Hire professionals: DIY electrical work is dangerous and illegal in Kenya.
